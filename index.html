Explore the Enchantment of Siquijor

Discover the magic of Siquijor, an island paradise known for its breathtaking beaches, mesmerizing waterfalls, and mystical charm. Whether you're seeking adventure, relaxation, or a spiritual retreat, Siquijor has something special for every traveler.

featured places

Cambugahay Fall

Cambugahay Falls is a breathtaking multi-tiered waterfall surrounded by lush greenery. Its crystal-clear turquoise waters offer a serene escape, perfect for swimming, swinging on rope swings, and enjoying nature's beauty. A must-visit spot for relaxation and adventure!

Lagaan Fall

Lagaan Falls, nestled in the heart of Siquijor Island, is a hidden gem surrounded by verdant forests. Its cascading waters create a refreshing natural pool, ideal for swimming and unwinding. With its tranquil ambiance and picturesque setting, Lagaan Falls is perfect for nature lovers seeking a peaceful retreat.

Lugnason Fall

Lugnason Falls, located in the serene landscapes of Siquijor Island, is a captivating waterfall known for its refreshing waters and natural beauty. Surrounded by lush greenery, it's an ideal spot for swimming, relaxation, and connecting with nature's tranquility.

Paliton Beach

Paliton Beach is a stunning paradise on Siquijor Island, known for its white sand, crystal-clear waters, and picturesque palm trees. A serene spot perfect for swimming, sunset watching, and enjoying the beauty of an untouched tropical escape.

Salagdoong Beach

Tubod Beach

Tubod Beach is a tranquil haven on Siquijor Island, featuring powdery white sand and calm turquoise waters. It's ideal for snorkeling, thanks to its vibrant marine sanctuary filled with colorful coral reefs and diverse marine life.

Capilay Spring Park

Capilay Spring Park is a serene public park in Siquijor, featuring a natural spring pool surrounded by lush greenery. It's a popular spot for locals and tourists to relax, swim, and enjoy picnics amidst a refreshing natural setting.

Old Balete Tree

The Old Balete Tree is a mystical and iconic landmark in Siquijor, estimated to be over 400 years old. Beneath its sprawling roots lies a natural spring that feeds a fish spa, where visitors can enjoy a relaxing experience while soaking in the tree's enchanting atmosphere.
